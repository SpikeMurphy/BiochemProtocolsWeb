Procedure

Build the Chamber

  1. Degrease glass plates with EtOH 70%.
  2. Dry glass plates.
  3. Construct the gel cassette by bring together two glass plates with one black spacer at each of the short ends and fixing the plates and spacers with grey clamps so the long edges of the plates are even with each other and the spacers.
  4. Insert gel cassette into the casting stand and fix secure them with the black screws.
  5. Use dsH2O to test for leaks.
  6. Optionally, remove the dsH2O with paper towels.

Separating Gel

  1. Prepare the separating gel and the collecting gel (TEMED and APS catalyze the polymerization).
Gel Polymerization
‚ö†Ô∏è Both gels may be prepared in advance. However, TEMED and APS must only be added immediately before use, as their addition initiates polymerization.
  1. Finish preparing the separating gel by adding TEMED and APS.
‚ö†Ô∏è Use immediately.
  1. Pipette the appropriate volume (11 mL) of separating gel between the glass plates.
‚ö†Ô∏è Ensure sufficient space at the top of the gel to accommodate the well comb, leaving a gap of approximately 0.5‚Äì1 cm between the comb and the gel surface.

  2. Let the gel polymerize @ RT for at least 30‚Äì45 minutes.

Running the Gel

  1. Carefully remove the well comb.
  2. Add cathode buffer on top of the gel.
  3. Pour out cathode buffer.
  4. Add cathode buffer on top of the gel.
  5. Add 10 ¬µL protein ladder in the first and last pocket.
  6. Add 20 ¬µL prepared sample per pocket.
  7. Document the loading scheme.
  8. Add electrophoresis chamber top on top of the glass plates.
  9. Remove the black screws from the casting stand and fix electrophoresis chamber top to the gel cassette with the black screws.
  10. Add approximately 2500 mL of the running buffer into the electrophoresis tank.
  11. Position the electrophoresis chamber in the electrophoresis tank.
  1. Fill the electrophoresis chamber with approximately 500 mL of cathode buffer until the electrodes are submerged but pay attention to the maximum filling marks.
  2. Run the gel @ 120V for approximately 40 minutes or until the samples are in the collecting gel.
