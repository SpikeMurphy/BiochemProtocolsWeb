Anhydrotetracycline Hydrochloride

Spike Murphy Müller ¹
¹ Center for Experimental Medicine, Institute of Biochemistry and Signal Transduction, Working Group Signal Transduction in Cancer
METADATA
Identity
Name
Anhydrotetracycline
Short name
AHT
IUPAC name
hydrogen (2Z)-2-[amino(hydroxy)methylidene]-4-(dimethylamino)-11,12a-dihydroxy-6-methyl-1,2,3,4,4a,5,12,12a-octahydrotetracene-1,3,12-trione chloride
Other name
Anhydrotetracycline Hydrochloride
CAS number
13803-65-1
Composition & structure
Molecular formula
C22H23ClN2O7
Molecular weight
462.88 g/mol

SAFETY
Global Harmonized System
Signal word
danger
Health hazard
acute toxicity, serious eye damage, eye irritation, reproductive toxicity
Personal protection
Gloves
nitrile gloves, EN ISO 374-1 type B
Eye protection
recommended
Mask
not normally required; use dust mask if powder handling generates aerosols
Fume hood
not required

Always try to minimize formation of dust and aerosols.
Always dispose of chemical waste according to institutional and local regulations.
Always consult institutional safety documentation and safety data sheet before use.
HANDLING
Physical properties
Appearance
yellow crystalline solid
Melting point
not determined, decomposes
Boiling point
not applicable, decomposes before boiling
Solubility
~5 g/L in water, soluble in DMSO, ethanol, DMF, methanol
Effective pH range
2-9
Acid/base properties
amphoteric compound with multiple weakly acidic and basic functional groups
Net charge
approximately neutral at pH 7
Stability & storage
Stability
photosensitive, moisture sensitive, degrades upon prolonged exposure to light and moisture
Storage conditions
-20 °C, protected from light, dry and tightly sealed, optimally under inert gas (argon)
Shelf life
at least 1 year under proper storage conditions
Compatibility & Use
Typical stocks
from 21.6 µM (0.01 mg/mL), up to 4.32 mM (2 mg/mL)
Preparation
Full preparation and storage instructions: Anhydrotetracycline Hydrochloride Stock 4.2 mM
Avoid contact with
strong acids, strong bases bases, strong oxidizing agents, light exposure
