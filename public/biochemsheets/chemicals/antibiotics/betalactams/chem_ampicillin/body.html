Ampicillin Sodium Salt

Spike Murphy Müller ¹
¹ Center for Experimental Medicine, Institute of Biochemistry and Signal Transduction, Working Group Signal Transduction in Cancer
METADATA
Identity
Name
Ampicillin Sodium
Short name
Amp, Amp-Na
IUPAC name
sodium (2S,5R,6R)-6-[(2R)-2-amino-2-phenylacetamido]-3,3-dimethyl-7-oxo-4-thia-1-azabicyclo[3.2.0]heptane-2-carboxylic acid
Other name
Ampicillin, α-Aminobenzylpenicillin sodium, Ampicillin sodium salt
CAS number
69-52-3
Composition & structure
Molecular formula
C16H18N3O4S
Molecular weight
371.39 g/mol

SAFETY
Global Harmonized System
Signal word
Danger
Health hazard
respiratory sensitization, skin sensitization
Personal protection
Gloves
nitrile gloves, EN ISO 374-1 type B
Eye protection
not normally required
Mask
not normally required; use dust mask if powder handling generates aerosols
Fume hood
not required

Always try to minimize formation of dust and aerosols.
Always dispose of chemical waste according to institutional and local regulations.
Always consult institutional safety documentation and safety data sheet before use.
HANDLING
Physical properties
Appearance
white to off-white powder
Melting point
215 °C, decomposes at ≥ 215 °C
Solubility
~40 g/L in water, mostly insoluble in most organic solvents
pKa
pKa₁ ≈ 2.5 (carboxyl), pKa₂ ≈ 7.2 (amine)
Effective pH range
5.5–7.5
Acid/base properties
weak acid and weak base with buffering near pH 7
Net charge
zwitterionic near pH 7, negatively charged above ~7.2, positively charged below ~7.2
Isoelectric point
pI ≈ 5.3
Stability & storage
Stability
photosensitive, stable in air, hydrolyzes in aqueous solution, looses 65 % of its initial activity when stored in Water at 4 °C for seven days
Storage conditions
4 °C, protected from light
Shelf life
2–3 years under proper storage conditions
Compatibility & Use
Typical stocks
50 mg/mL in Water, 100 mg/mL in Water
Preparation
Full preparation and storage instructions: Ampicillin Stock 100 mg/mL
Avoid contact with
strong acids, strong bases, oxidizing agents, β-lactamase enzymes
