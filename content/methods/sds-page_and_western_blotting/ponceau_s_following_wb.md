## Procedure

{{< infobox type="info" title="General remark" >}}
⚠️ Ponceau S staining needs to be done prior to antibody incubation.
{{< /infobox >}}

### Heading 1

1. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare a plastic tray with dsH<sub>2</sub>O on a tray shaker.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Place the nitrocellulose membrane into the dsH<sub>2</sub>O and gently agitate for 2 minutes.

{{< timer time="0:02:00" >}}

3. {{< checkbox >}} Change the dsH<sub>2</sub>O and gently agitate for 2 minutes.

{{< timer time="0:02:00" >}}

4. {{< checkbox >}} Replace the dsH<sub>2</sub>O with approximately 50 mL of Ponceau S solution and gently agitate for 5 minutes or until protein bands become visible.

{{< timer time="0:05:00" >}}

5. {{< checkbox >}} Transfer the Ponceau S solution into a sterile 50 mL falcon tube for reuse.
6. {{< checkbox >}} Add fresh dsH<sub>2</sub>O to the nitrocellulose membrane and gently agitate for 5 minutes.

{{< timer time="0:05:00" >}}

7. {{< checkbox >}} Change the dsH<sub>2</sub>O and gently agitate for 5 minutes or until background is sufficiently reduced.

{{< timer time="0:05:00" >}}

8. {{< checkbox >}} Take an epilumination picture at an appropriate imager.

{{< infobox type="info" title="Time Remark" >}}
⚠️ Image immediately as Ponceau S fades quickly over time.
{{< /infobox >}}

6. {{< checkbox >}} Replace the dsH<sub>2</sub>O in the tray with TBST or PBST.
9. {{< checkbox >}} Place the nitrocellulose membrane into the TBST or PBST and gently agitate for 5 minutes.

{{< timer time="0:05:00" >}}

3. {{< checkbox >}} Change the TBST or PBST and gently agitate for 5 minutes or until the nitrocellulose membrane appears completely white again.
