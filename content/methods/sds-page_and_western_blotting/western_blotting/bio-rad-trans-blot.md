## Procedure

### Preparations the Day Before

1. {{< checkbox >}} If the SDS-PAGE gel was previously stained with a Coomassie stain, such as Roti®Blue quick, destain in EtOH 30 % @ RT for 30 minutes or @ 4 °C overnight.

{{< timer time="0:30:00" >}}

2. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare 2.5l of transfer buffer by mixing 1750 mL of dsH<sub>2</sub>O with 500 mL of MeOH and 250 mL of 10x Western Blot tris-glycine(-SDS) Towbin transfer buffer.
3. {{< checkbox >}} Let the transfer buffer cool down to 4 °C overnight.

### Pre-Transfer Preparations

1. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare a small tray with transfer buffer to fit the gel.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare a small tray with dsH<sub>2</sub>O to fit the nitrocellulose membrane.
3. {{< checkbox >}} Place the SDS-PAGE gel into the tray with transfer buffer.

{{< infobox type="info" title="" >}}
⚠️ Some Coomassie stains, such as Roti®Blue quick, tend to bind SDS, so the gel needs to be equilibrated in transfer buffer containing of at least 0.1 % SDS for 15 minutes.

{{< timer time="0:15:00" >}}

{{< /infobox >}}

4. {{< checkbox >}} Cut a nitrocellulose membrane per gel to the size of the SDS-PAGE separating gel.
5. {{< checkbox >}} Remove the protective foil from the nitrocellulose membrane.
6. {{< checkbox >}} Place the nitrocellulose membrane into dsH<sub>2</sub>O.

{{< infobox type="info" title="Nitrocellulose" >}}
⚠️ Do not allow the exposed membrane to contact any surface other than water. Handle only with forceps, gripping the edges.
{{< /infobox >}}

7. {{< checkbox >}} Cut two filter papers per gel to the size of the SDS-PAGE separating gel.
8. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare a large tray with transfer buffer to fit the gel holder cassette.

### Constructing the Gel Holder Cassette

1. {{< checkbox >}} Place the gel holder cassette with the cathode facing side (usually black side) down into the tray with transfer buffer.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Place a blotting sponge on top of the cathode facing side (usually black side) of the gel holder cassette and also soak the second sponge in transfer buffer.
3. {{< checkbox >}} Place the first filter paper onto the sponge.
4. {{< checkbox >}} Place SDS-PAGE separating gel onto the first filter paper.
5. {{< checkbox >}} Place nitrocellulose membrane onto the SDS-PAGE gel.

{{< infobox type="info" title="Nitrocellulose" >}}
⚠️ Don’t adjust the placement of the membrane, because the transfer starts immediately.
{{< /infobox >}}

6. {{< checkbox >}} Place the second filter paper onto the nitrocellulose membrane.
7. {{< checkbox >}} Roll over the filter paper to remove all bubbles.

{{< infobox type="info" title="Nitrocellulose" >}}
⚠️ Be careful not to move the gel and nitrocellulose membrane. Do not exert unnecessary pressure to avoid rupturing the gel.
{{< /infobox >}}

8. {{< checkbox >}} Place the second blotting sponge onto the second filter paper.
9. {{< checkbox >}} Close the gel holder cassette.

### Preparing the Blotting Tank and Running the Blot

1. {{< checkbox >}} Add the remaining transfer buffer to the blotting tank and add a magnetic stir bar.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Place the gel holder cassette in the blotting tank so the membrane is oriented towards the anode (red) in relation to the gel and the gel is oriented towards the cathode (black) in relation to the membrane (black to black).
3. {{< checkbox >}} Fill up the tank with the transfer buffer from the trays until the buffer level is high enough to cover where the gel is placed in the gel holder cassette.
4. {{< checkbox >}} Place the lid onto the tank.
5. {{< checkbox >}} Place the chamber onto a magnetic stirrer in the cold room.
6. {{< checkbox >}} Connect the power source with the correct polarity and blot @ 65 V for 1-4 h (default 2 h).

{{< infobox type="info" title="Blotting duration" >}}
⚠️ Transfer duration depends on protein size.

| Size | Approximate Duration |
| :-- | :-- |
| < 20 kDa | 1 h @ 65 V |
| 20-50 kDa | 1-2 h @ 65 V |
| 50-100 kDa | 2 h @ 65 V |
| 100-200 kDa | 3-4 h @ 65 V or overnight @ 20-30 V |

### Post-Transfer

1. {{< checkbox >}} Remove the gel holder cassette from the blotting tank and disassemble the cassette.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Mark the membrane directly after removing the gel, e.g. on the top right, to always know its orientation.
3. {{< checkbox >}} Wash the membrane for 2 minutes in dsH<sub>2</sub>O.
