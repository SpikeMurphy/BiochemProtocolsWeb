## Procedure

### Preparations

1. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare and pre-cool 10 mL [Tris-HCl-NaCl-MgCl<sub>2</sub> bacterial lysis buffer]({{< relref "bacterial_lysis_buffer.md" >}}) as a equibrilation and wash buffer per 5 mL bacterial lysate from 500 mL bacterial culture volume (or 10 mL buffer W).
2. {{< checkbox >}} Prepare and pre-cool 1 mL [biotin elution buffer]({{< relref "biotin_elution_buffer.md" >}}) per 5 mL bacterial lysate from 500 mL bacterial culture volume (or 1 mL buffer BXT).

### Equilibrating the Column

1. {{< checkbox >}} Place the column onto a stand in the cold room @ 2-8 °C.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Remove the top cap.

{{< infobox type="info" title="" >}}
⚠️ Keep the top cap for resealing and reuse.
{{< /infobox >}}

3. {{< checkbox >}} Twist of bottom cap.
4. {{< checkbox >}} Let the storage buffer flow through.
5. {{< checkbox >}} Equilibrate the column with 1 column bed volume (200 µL) of wash buffer (or buffer W).
6. {{< checkbox >}} Equilibrate the column with 1 column bed volume (200 µL) of wash buffer (or buffer W).

### First Storage

1. {{< checkbox >}} Add 500 µL glycerol 85 % (approximately 615 mg) to a sterile 1.5 mL reaction tube.

{{< infobox type="info" title="" >}}
⚠️ Pipetting highly viscous glycerol can be challenging and may lead to inaccurate measurements. Micropipettes with cut of pipette tips can make pipetting easier. Weighing is often a more reliable alternative.
{{< /infobox >}}

2. {{< checkbox >}} After the final elution add the eluate to the prepared glycerol for a final concentration of 38.6 % (4.93 M).
3. Rotate end-over-end for 5-10 minutes.

<div style="display:flex; gap:20px; align-items:center;">
{{< timer time="0:05:00" >}}
{{< timer time="0:10:00" >}}
</div>

4. {{< checkbox >}} Take a 4 µL aliquot + 4 µL dsH<sub>2</sub>O and a 8 µL aliquot and mix them with  10 µL of 2x Laemmli sample buffer and freeze @ -20 °C for later SDS-PAGE analysis.

{{< infobox type="info" title="SDS-PAGE" >}}
⚠️ The samples will be mixed fresh with 2µL DTT for a final concentration of the sample buffer of 1x. Two dilutions give more opportunity to have an adequate amount of purified protein in the SDS-PAGE analysis, when doing concentration calculations with a BSA standard.
{{< /infobox >}}

5. {{< checkbox >}} Optionally aliquot the rest in 50–100 µL fractions.
6. {{< checkbox >}} Store @ –20 °C for middle term storage or –80 °C for long term storage.

### First Column regeneration

{{< infobox type="info" title="" >}}
⚠️ The column can be reused >10 times according to the manufacturer. It is not recommended to purify different proteins on one column. Reuse only when purifying the same protein again.
{{< /infobox >}}

1. {{< checkbox >}} Add 15 column bed volumes (3000 µL) of fresh NaOH 100 mM to the column.
2. {{< checkbox >}} Let the NaOH pass through completely.
3. {{< checkbox >}} Wash column immediately with at least 15 column bed volumes (3000 µL) of wash buffer (or buffer W).
